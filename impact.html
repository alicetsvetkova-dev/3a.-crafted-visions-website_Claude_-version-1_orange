Impact

The crafts we use are the crafts we keep alive.

Hand-weaving, pottery, calligraphy and bookbinding are practiced today by a shrinking number of people in Tunisia. Every session we run is paid work for one of them, the actual transaction your booking is, not a donation on the side.

How it works

Where your money actually goes.

No. 1

Paid directly, per session

The artisan’s fee is fixed into the price before we take any margin. It is not a tip or a bonus, it is their primary reason for taking the booking.

No. 2

Practiced, not displayed

Teaching a technique is one of the few ways a craft with shrinking demand stays exercised and passed on, rather than archived behind museum glass.

No. 3

Documented for you

For company bookings we record which artisan you worked with and what the session produced: real material for a CSR report, not a vague claim.

For your CSR report

A team-building that also counts as impact spend.

CSR, Corporate Social Responsibility, is the social and community impact a company is expected to show and report. If yours already tracks that spend, a Crafted Visions engagement fits the line cleanly: named local artisans paid directly, a documented craft kept in practice, and a team outcome you were budgeting for anyway.

After the event we prepare a short summary for your internal reporting, so the impact is on record, not just felt in the room.
