Bookbinding

Fold, stitch and press your own book with one of the last traditional bookbinders in Tunis.

Book this workshop All workshops
The craft

One of the last bookbinders in Tunis.

In a historic atelier in the Medina, you learn to make a book the old way, folding the pages, stitching the spine by hand and pressing the finished block, guided by a bookbinder practicing a craft that is quietly disappearing.

By the end you hold something genuinely useful and entirely yours, a notebook to write, sketch or give, made by your own hands.

Why it’s good for you

The calm of making by hand.

No. 1

Rhythmic and meditative

The repetitive motion of stitching works like meditation: breathing slows, the heart rate steadies and stress eases. It is a hands-on way to quiet a busy head.

No. 2

Patience and precision

Every fold and stitch rewards care and attention to detail. Bookbinding is a gentle lesson in slowing down and doing one thing well.

No. 3

A real thing at the end

You walk out with a finished, handmade book, and the quiet pride that comes with it, while helping keep an endangered craft in living practice.

The session

Ready to bind your own book?

About 3 hours in a historic atelier in the Medina of Tunis. Small group, maximum 6 people. All materials, snacks and drinks included, no experience needed. 49 € per person, and your book comes home with you.
